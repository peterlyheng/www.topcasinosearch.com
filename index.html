Fiji Online Gambling Laws and Regulations

Let's take a look at the online gambling laws, rules, and regulations in Fiji. We'll find out if it's legal, what age you need to be to gamble online, and if there are any taxes on your winning.

Is online gambling allowed in Fiji?

Online gambling in Fiji has been legal since 2009 under the 2009 Fiji Gambling Act (sections 27 – 29).

However, it's crucial to use only licensed online casino websites. Running unlicensed online casinos in Fiji is illegal, and playing on such sites is also against the law. If caught, individuals involved in unlicensed online gaming may face penalties. This includes fines up to $5,000 and a maximum prison term of 2 years.

In Fiji, there isn't a dedicated gambling authority. Instead, the President handles licenses for gambling businesses. If you want to start one, you'll need to contact the government. For details on fees, how to apply, and the documents required, you can check out the Gaming (Forms and Fees) Regulations from 2009.

What is the Legal Online Gambling Age in Fiji?

Most international casinos that accept Fiji players expect them to be at least 18 years old. This is a common age restriction in many countries with legal gambling.

Does Fiji tax Online casino winnings?

No, players from Fiji are not taxed on their winnings. The online casinos are taxed by the government, and this tax is determined by the Gambling Turnover Tax Decree.
