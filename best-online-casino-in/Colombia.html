Laws and Regulations for Online Gambling in Colombia

Colombia has laws that make the Colombian gambling industry safe and regulated. In this guide, we'll discuss whether online gambling in Colombia is legal, the age at which you can gamble, and taxes on casino winnings in Colombia.

Are Online Gambling Sites Legal in Colombia?

Colombia has set up clear rules for regulating online gambling, with Colijuegos overseeing the market. In 2016, Colijuegos made a significant move by expanding its control to online casinos. Before this, Colijuegos mainly regulated land-based gambling, starting with the Baloto Lottery. With these new regulations, Colombia became the first South American nation to legalize online betting.

To operate in the Colombian gambling market, online operators must get a valid license from Colijuegos. These licenses last five years and come with strict rules from Law 643 of 2001 and the eGaming Act of 2016. There is also an annual review to ensure operators follow the rules.

In addition to licensing fees, which are $200,000 per year, operators must pay taxes based on their earnings. In 2017, the Colombian online gambling industry started giving out licenses.

In conclusion, Online gambling in Colombia is legal. Players can pick their choice of Colombian online casinos to start winning.

What is the Legal gambling age in Colombia?

You must be at least 18 years old to gamble online in Colombia. The age requirement ensures that only legal adults can engage in online betting. This makes the gambling environment safer and more secure for all Colombian players.

Does Colombia collect taxes on Online casino winnings?

In 2019, the gambling sector brought in significant tax revenue for Colijuegos. They made a total revenue of COL$ 680 million, a 16% increase from the COL$ 574 million in 2018.

The current tax on gross revenue from online gambling is 15%, which applies to all operators, whether online or in physical locations. An extra 1% tax goes to the regulatory body. There are exceptions, though. Operators providing games of skill don't have to pay this tax. They get a tax break, encouraging them to offer these skill-based games. But operators with games of luck must pay their taxes on time.

If operators don't fulfill their tax obligations, especially on gross revenue, they could face hefty fines or even lose their gambling license. Colijuegos and the Colombian government take decisive steps to find and punish operators who don't follow tax rules.

Therefore, the answer to the question is yes. Colombia does collect taxes on casino winnings, but only for chance games.
