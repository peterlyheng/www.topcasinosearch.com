Online Gambling Laws and Regulations in Andorra

Are Online Casinos Legal in Andorra?

Digital casinos are legally permitted in Andorra, regulated by Gambling Law 4/20211, replacing 37/2014.

To operate within the country, an internet casino operator must obtain a license from the Consell Regulador Andorrà del Joc (CRAJ), Andorra's licensing authority.

In Andorra, the legalization of online casino gambling came after other forms of online gambling due to the challenge of effective regulation.

Andorra, not an EU member, does not adhere to the usual EU laws regarding electronic gambling, as they do not apply within the country.

What is Andorra's Online Casino Licensing and Regulatory Body?

The Consell Regulador Andorrà del Joc (CRAJ) is the licensing authority for online gambling in Andorra. Those interested in establishing any gambling-related business within the country must contact this authority. Website access and licensing information are available exclusively in Catalan.

History of Gambling in Andorra: Recent Legal Struggles

Andorra saw the legalization of gambling in 1996, initially with bingo. In 2012, bingo halls with machines emerged, followed by the legalization of casinos in 2018. The country, known for its tourist appeal, anticipates a potential tourism surge with the introduction of a casino.

Casinos became legal in Andorra in 2018, regulated under Gambling Law 4/20211. Since then, a casino operator requires a license to run a brick-and-mortar business. However, the inauguration of the first casino in Andorra was slated for 2022, despite the legalization.

Following the legalization in 2018, expectations rose for Andorra to house a casino. The CRAJ declared that only serious, long-term candidates would be considered, and the initial casino license would span 20 years.

After years of deliberation, a casino license was finally granted to Jocs S.A., set to operate Andorra's first casino, Casino Andorra at Prat de la Creu, scheduled for opening in 2022.

The legal battle highlighted various flaws in the new casino regulations, prompting the government to temporarily suspend casino licensing until these issues from the trial are rectified.

The legalization of casino gambling held significant importance for the small European country, given that tourism contributes around 80% to its GDP, with casinos and resorts being recognized for their potential to boost tourism.
