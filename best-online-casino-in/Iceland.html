Here are some of the Roulette types you can find in Iceland - 

  • American Roulette features a wheel with 38 pockets, including a zero and a double zero, offering a unique betting layout and a higher house edge.

Legal Status Summary for Online Gambling in Iceland

Online casinos are not legally permitted in Iceland. Icelandic citizens can legally play on off-shore licensed online casinos. The European Economic Area (EEA) legislation is applicable in Iceland and protects players.

The European Union does not enforce changes in local laws about casinos. But, it advocates for legislation in member countries, including Iceland. According to the General Penal Code (Article 183 and 184), most forms of gambling and income derived from it are illegal in Iceland. Exceptions exist for gambling establishments that donate all profits to charity. The University of Iceland and Íslandsspil are two such licensed establishments.

In 2020, the Icelandic Government launched a plan describing steps for a safer gambling environment for players. including oversight by the Money Laundering Unit of the Directorate of Internal Revenue. Despite ongoing debates in the Icelandic parliament. There are currently no significant changes expected in Iceland's gambling legislation.

While gambling is illegal in Iceland, many reputable online gambling platforms will accept Icelandic players without legal risk.
