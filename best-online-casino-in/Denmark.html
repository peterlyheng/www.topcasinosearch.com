The Current Situation with Gambling in Denmark

  • The Danish gambling market underwent liberalization on January 1st, 2012.
  • Strict regulations are upheld by the DGA.
  • The legal gambling age in Denmark is 18, applicable to both online and land-based casinos, regardless of citizenship.

What kind of Licenses Exist in the Danish Gambling Market

Denmark offers two distinct online gambling licenses: 

  1. betting license, which also includes brick-and-mortar casinos
  2. online casino license.

Online gambling operators are required to pay an annual fee for their licenses, with the DGA having the authority to grant full licenses, conditional licenses, or reject applications.

Brick-and-mortar casinos in Denmark must also hold a license issued by the Danish Gambling Authority. Presently, only seven casinos hold such licenses, and new operators cannot currently apply for one.

Is Online Gambling Legal in Denmark?

Gambling is entirely legal in Denmark, encompassing casinos, bookmakers, and lotteries.

Both domestic and foreign operators can apply for remote gaming licenses to cater to Danish players, with non-compliance leading to serious consequences.

What is the Danish Gambling Authority and Its Mandate?

Spillemyndigheden, the Danish Gambling Authority (DGA) is the government that oversees and regulates every online casino in Denmark, along with other online gaming providers.

The DGA even has a role in approving casino managers and the entire casino staff in Denmark Casinos.

What is the Legal Gambling Age for Danish Players?

Danish players must be at least 18 years old to participate in land-based casinos or online gambling industry, increased to 21 years old for operators.

Taxation on Gambling Winnings in Denmark

In Denmark, gambling winnings are generally tax-free for players. This means that you do not need to pay taxes on your winnings if you win money from gambling, whether at a real or online casino. The Danish government does not consider gambling winnings taxable income, and players get to keep 100% of their winnings.

However, there is an important exception to this rule. If you are a professional gambler and your primary source of income is gambling, then your winnings may be subject to taxation. In this case, you would need to report your gambling income to the tax authorities, which would be taxed as regular income according to Denmark's standard income tax rates.

For the vast majority of recreational gamblers, though, there are no taxes on gambling winnings in Denmark, making it a favorable environment for those who enjoy casino games and betting.

History of Gambling in Denmark

The Danish gambling industry, as we know it today, took shape in 2000 with the establishment of the Spillemyndigheden, marking the onset of a fully regulated sector.

However, gambling has deep roots in Denmark, dating back to the 19th century when it became a fashionable pastime, particularly in Copenhagen.

Notably, the Casino Marienlyst in Helsingor has been in business for approximately 200 years, showcasing Denmark's enduring gambling heritage.

Outline of Events in History of Gambling in Denmark

  • Danske Spil, an authorized lottery ticket seller, held the gambling monopoly in Denmark from 1948 to 2008 when legislative changes opened the market to external gambling sites.
  • The Danish Gambling Act comprises 13 sections defining its purpose, legal terms, licensing criteria, advertising rules, and the Danish Gambling Authority (DGA) supervision.
  • The market further liberalized in 2012, with online gambling laws allowing foreign casino operators to offer their services online, leading to increased competition and government crackdowns on illicit operators.
