Online Gambling Laws and Regulations in Luxembourg

Gambling is not a legal activity in Luxembourg. There are a few exceptions to the laws that let you play casino games at the Casino 2000, located in Mondorf. However, generally speaking, the gambling law dating back to April 1977 says gambling is prohibited. 

In addition to Casino 2000, the national lottery is also operating. They both are operating under amendments to the law created in 2017. These changes in the law enabled some exceptions to the law from 1977.

The power in numbers - Luxembourg players exist.

The government created this change because Luxembourg is a relatively wealthy country. And its citizens are, in large numbers, gambling enthusiasts who like playing for a lot of money. 

Compared to other countries throughout Europe, the people in Luxembourg spend a lot more on gambling. Today, there are no rules regulating the online industry. As long as no laws exist to regulate the market, the government losses enormous sums in possible tax collection and licensing fees.

Where can you play online? 

Luxembourg players can safely play the most reputable and safe online casinos. However, the country suffers significant losses from these activities. Some legislators in Luxembourg consider this matter, and on occasion, there are discussions on this subject in government institutions. 

Changes in the current gambling legislation will likely occur in upcoming years. As of 2023, the government plans to start regulating the online gambling industry and is considering a few first brands to operate within the country. The land-based sector in Luxembourg is thriving and is very profitable. Making the change online is only logical. We will remain updated on such changes. 

Current Legislation in Luxembourg

In the eyes of past regulators, gambling is considered a destructive activity. This is the main reason why gambling has been prohibited since April 1977. This law has stayed the same over time. However, the exceptions made are a step in the right direction.

Casino 2000 is the main establishment allowing gambling in Luxembourg. To approve its operation, Casino 2000 had to create a responsible gambling program that proved very thorough and helpful over the years. Many countries throughout Europe learned and tried to duplicate this program. 

What types of gambling are mentioned in the law, and what rules apply to each of them?

Poker - 

Luxembourg allows four types of poker games, including Small stakes poker, Stud poker, Texas Hold'em poker (since 2015), and Omaha poker (since 2015). These games are only permitted in casinos. In 2007, the Luxembourg public prosecutor's department announced a gentlemen's agreement between the Ministry of Justice and poker organizers, which allows low-stake poker events (for any type of poker) in bars if certain conditions are met, such as the stakes paid by players being low (about €20 to €50 per player, depending on the organizer's expenses). This agreement aims to prevent addiction and money laundering.

Sports betting - 

The government in Luxembourg allows sports betting and permits companies that want to offer it. The rules for sports betting are written in a document called the Grand-Ducal Regulation of 7 September 1987. This document explains the conditions for getting permission, how the bets work, and how much tax needs to be paid. In Luxembourg, there are no limits on the kinds of sports you can bet on, and you can choose from different types of bets like betting on the winner, live betting, and more.

Casino games - 

Only one casino in Luxembourg has permission to offer different types of games. The casino is called Casino 2000 Montdorf. They can offer games like roulette, blackjack, craps, punto banco, and others. These games are regulated by the government, which means they have rules they must follow. If someone else wants to open a casino, they need to get permission from the government and follow specific rules. The government will investigate and decide if opening a new casino is okay.

Are there any differences in how mobile gambling and interactive gambling on television are regulated?

In Luxembourg, no special rules apply only to mobile gambling or interactive gambling on TV. The same restrictions for all gambling types apply, even on a mobile phone or TV. The government considers gambling as part of criminal law. Hence, the same rules for any gambling activities apply to services offered by a mobile or television provider in Luxembourg or aimed at people living in Luxembourg, no matter where the provider is located.

How much is blockchain technology used in gambling in Luxembourg? And how does the government regulate it?

In Luxembourg, there are no special rules for blockchain-based gambling. The government has made rules about how to use blockchain for securities (like stocks and bonds) to help banks and others use this technology. These rules say that banks can use blockchain technology to manage securities accounts. Other than that, there are no other special rules for blockchain, except for the law that requires companies that deal with virtual assets (like cryptocurrencies that use the blockchain) to follow rules to stop money laundering and terrorism. This law refers specifically to companies that manage, transfer, or exchange virtual assets and must follow these rules.

Online Gambling History in Luxembourg

Gambling history in the country goes way back to the 1870s when the first official gambling licenses were issued. 

  • The 1870s - First gambling licenses were issued
  • The 1880s - First land-based gambling establishment was formed.
  • 1888 - Lottery was legalized.
  • 1900 (roughly) until 1977 - Gambling is not allowed in Luxembourg as well as most of Europe
  • 1977 - Casinos were legalized again in the country, and this law holds until this day. 
  • 1987 - Sports betting is legalized
