Can You Legally Gamble in Montenegro?

Absolutely, gambling is legal in Montenegro. In fact, the country really encourages residents and visitors to have fun with gambling while also putting in place protections for everyone. Since their laws around gambling are pretty new, they've been able to regulate both real-world and online gaming pretty effectively.
Their rules around getting a license to operate a casino are pretty clear, which is something other countries struggling with regulation could learn from. Montenegro only allows eight real-world casinos to operate at any given time.
Casinos can get a license by winning a competitive bidding process set up by the government. These licenses last for ten years and can be extended by five years. A casino license also lets operators have slot machines in their casinos without any extra fees.
If you want to operate slot machines, you need a three-year license, which can be extended by two years. Bingo owners can get a license for three years. Bookies can get a license for three years, with the option to extend for two years, but they can't make bets on political elections. Similarly, sports team owners can't create bets for the outcomes of their own team's games.
To get an online license, you just need to show that you own a physical casino. Once you do that, you get a license without needing more documents or payments. This is a huge win for online casinos in Montenegro.
Every operator has to meet certain financial obligations based on the type of gambling they're offering. These clear laws make Montenegro one of the safest places to gamble.

History of Gambling in Montenegro

Montenegro, being its own country, hasn't had gambling for that long since it only started in 2006. But, it was always part of the scene when it was a part of other countries. When it was with Yugoslavia, people were allowed to gamble, but the government had a tight grip on it because they used it to make some money.
While Montenegro was with Serbia, it's unclear what the gambling scene looked like. It was still legal, but there were no clear rules about it. People also didn't agree on whether gambling should be legal in the early 2000s.
This led to Montenegro's parliament passing the 'Laws on Games of Chance' on July 28, 2004. This law is the foundation for all the other gambling laws in the country.
When Montenegro became its own country in 2006, the question wasn't whether gambling should be allowed. People were already cool with it. The real question was how to put some rules around it. Around the same time, online gaming was becoming more popular. This allowed the government to easily regulate real-world and online casinos in Montenegro.
