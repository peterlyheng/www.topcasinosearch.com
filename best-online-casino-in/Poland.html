    Online Gambling Licensing Laws and Regulations in Poland

    The Polish Act on Gambling of 2009 regulates online gambling in Poland. The regulation has, with time, undergone changes to introduce strict measures with which online gambling is controlled in Poland. Some of the important aspects of the online gambling laws include:

    The law regulating gambling in Poland is the Act of 19 November 2009 on gambling games (the "Gambling Act"). The regulation has, with time, changed to introduce strict measures. They explain how different kinds of gambling should be managed. Some of the essential aspects of the online gambling laws include:

    1. Types of Gambling Games: The Polish government considers only certain casino games legal. These include casino games, Poker, and sports betting. Other types of games are either banned or made available under strict licensing. So, online casinos that wish to offer these games must have the required license or only offer authorized games. Also, the Gambling Act divides gambling into games of chance, betting, card games, and slot machines. Games of chance include lotteries and games based primarily on luck. Betting means wagering on sports or other events. Card games include poker and similar games. Slot machines are stand-alone gaming devices. Each game type has its rules to ensure fairness and proper supervision.
    2. Licensing Requirements: Certain requirements must be met before an operator can operate a legal online casino in Poland. For example, the operator must have a registered office or branch in Poland. Then, they must only use customer service representatives who speak Polish. For betting products, you need a permit. Some gambling activities only require a notification to start. This system checks that operators meet the standards set by the law, ensuring the market is fair and safe for consumers. Without a license or permit, gambling services cannot be legally offered.
    3. Licensing Process: To get a license, operators must provide certain documents. These documents may include proof of financial stability and a business plan. They must also be translated into Polish by a certified translator. Although the law says it should take six months to process these applications, it often takes longer due to detailed reviews. This careful process ensures only qualified operators enter the market.
    4. License Restrictions: Gambling companies' Shareholders and board members must have good reputations. They must obey rules against money laundering and terrorism financing. They must undergo background checks and make financial security deposits. These rules ensure the gambling market is clear and safe, which builds consumer trust.
    5. Advertising Restrictions: Advertising gambling activities are mostly banned, except for state monopoly products. Betting advertisements are allowed, but strict rules must be followed about content and timing. These restrictions prevent gambling from being glamorized. They must not present gambling as a way to solve financial problems; they should only show it for entertainment. Also, the laws protect vulnerable groups, like minors, from being influenced by gambling ads.
    6. Tax Obligations: Gambling operators must pay taxes that vary by game type. For example, machine games are taxed at 50%, and other betting activities are taxed at 12%. This gaming tax supports public services and infrastructure. The tax system ensures that the gambling industry contributes fairly to the economy.
    7. Social Responsibility: Operators must ensure only those aged 18 and above can gamble. They must have rules to stop addictive and underage gambling. These rules create a safe and responsible gambling atmosphere.
    8. Online Gambling: The state controls most online games except betting and special offer lotteries. Foreign operators must appoint a local representative, which ensures that online gambling follows Polish laws and protects Polish players.
    9. Anti-Money Laundering (AML): Gambling operators must have AML policies and show proof of compliance. They must also use payment methods that ensure funds used in gambling are legal. These measures prevent the gambling industry from being used for money laundering, keeping the financial system secure.

    History of Online Gambling in Poland

    The history of online gambling in Poland is closely linked to the development of the laws over the years. When online gambling was initially introduced to Poland, it was unregulated. Polish players were able to play the games without limits or considering repercussions.

    As the industry grew, the 2009 Gambling Act was introduced in Poland. This Act regulated all forms of gambling, including online gambling. It made it compulsory for online gambling operators to get a valid license from the Ministry of Finance. This reduced the high number of gambling operators that existed before the Act.

    In 2011, the Gambling Act of 2009 was amended. It introduced even more limited restrictions to online gambling. Unlicensed online casino operators were shut down. A punishment fine was also added for Polish players who engaged in illegal gambling activities.

    In 2016, the amendment targeted stricter licensing and higher taxation for online casino operators. Taxes were increased based on the operators' revenue. Then, the government continued to illegalize all forms of gambling games except sports betting.

    This amendment significantly changed the industry in 2017. The government increased the number of authorized gambling games, introduced a blocklist containing casino sites operating without authorization, and took stricter enforcement measures against illegal gambling sites.

    This amendment remains the latest. The Polish government has remained steadfast in keeping the online gambling industry under control. It does this by monitoring sites and taking enforcement measures against any breaches noticed.
