History of Gambling in Croatia

A Journey through Croatia's Gambling Evolution

Croatia's pioneering spirit in legalizing gambling emerged six decades ago in the 1960s, setting the stage for Eastern Europe's regulatory landscape. Initially, gambling was confined to specific football games, with no penalties for participation. However, it wasn't until 1991, following the enactment of the Games of Chance Law, that the gambling sector gained independence and experienced significant growth.

Driven by a burgeoning tourism industry, Croatia witnessed the establishment of opulent casinos within its picturesque landscapes. Names like Merit Casino Libertas and Rixos Libertas Dubrovnik adorned the vibrant coastal towns, attracting visitors and creating a vibrant gambling scene. This era, marked by favorable tax conditions, saw the rise of international casino operators, but a turning point arrived in 2009.

The introduction of the Game of Chance Act ushered in a new era, allowing land-based casinos to thrive while marking the exit of smaller operators due to the 20% VAT imposition. The dynamics shifted, with local entities like Super Sport and Prva Sportska Kladionica seizing dominance.

Fast-forward to Croatia's EU membership in 2013, aligning its gambling regulations with fellow member states. This alignment introduced license fees, fostering a flourishing gambling culture and enticing online gambling operators to explore the Croatian market.

Amidst this historical backdrop, Croatia's gambling law, though stringent, appears to be embracing a more adaptable future for online casinos, hinting at an evolving landscape with increasing opportunities.
