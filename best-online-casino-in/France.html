French Online Gambling Laws and Regulations

France has a rich history of gambling, with ties to royalty and the invention of iconic games. Recently, the country has implemented strict regulations, leading to a complex landscape for the online gambling industry. Here, we delve into the legal status, taxation on winnings, gambling history, key events, and future predictions for gambling in France.

Are online casinos legal in France?

Online gambling in France is legal but comes with restrictions. The France Gambling Act permits three primary forms of online gambling:

  1. online poker,
  2. lotteries, and
  3. sports betting activities.

However, popular casino games like video slots are prohibited within the country's online casinos. French players can still access such games on gambling platforms without legal repercussions. The regulatory bodies overseeing online gambling in France include ARJEL, Française des Jeux for lotteries, and Pari-Mutuel Urbain (PMU) for sports betting.

What is the legal gambling age for French players?

According to French online gambling law, the legal gambling age in France is 18.

Taxation on Gambling Winnings 

Online gambling France legislation imposes high taxes on online casinos, which has led to some operators leaving the French market. These taxes and stringent regulations have limited the variety of games available in licensed online casinos. Despite this, many French players prefer unlicensed online casinos offering online poker games, sports betting, French roulette, baccarat, and slots with favorable terms and payouts.

Gambling History in France

France boasts a remarkable gambling history, with contributions to casino gaming worldwide. Blaise Pascal, a French mathematician, invented the first roulette wheel, and baccarat originated in France. Additionally, the introduction of the Queen to card decks occurred in France, resulting in the now-standard 52-card deck.
