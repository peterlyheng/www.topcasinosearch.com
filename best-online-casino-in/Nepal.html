Laws About Gambling in Nepal

Gambling is illegal in Nepal, except for the national lottery, which is the only legal form of gambling in the country.

The gambling laws are governed by the Public Gambling Act of 1963. This law was established before the Internet became popular, so it does not cover online gambling. This puts online gambling in a legal grey area.

In 2014, the Nepalese government closed all the casinos in the country. They reopened eight in 2015, but they were limited to tourists. Nepali citizens are not allowed to play at the land-based casinos in their country, and there are no licensed online casinos in Nepal.

This has led many Nepali gambling enthusiasts to turn to international online casinos. The good news is that many casino websites are accessible to Nepali players. Because this industry is unregulated, players must be careful and play only at reputable, licensed online casinos.

The Legal Online Gambling Age in Nepal

Online gambling is unregulated in Nepal, so the government does not set age limits. Most online casinos don’t accept players under 18. You must, therefore, be above 18 to play at licensed offshore casino sites.

Taxations on Online Gambling Winnings in Nepal

Naturally, online gambling winnings are not subject to taxes because the government does not regulate this industry. However, in the event of huge cashouts, we advise consulting with a tax expert in your country.

History of Gambling in Nepal

The first land-based casino in Nepal opened its doors in 1968. Starting the unique relationship between the country and gambling. Most forms of gambling are illegal, including online poker rooms and sports betting platforms. Nepal has taken a more relaxed approach towards tourists. Visitors are free to gamble in Nepal's 8 brick-and-mortar casinos. A decision that contributes to the country's economy. This makes Nepal one of the few South Asian countries that issue licenses for land-based casinos. Unlike its neighboring countries, there is also a national lottery available.
