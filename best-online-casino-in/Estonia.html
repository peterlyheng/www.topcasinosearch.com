Is Online Gambling Legal in Estonia?

Yes, online gambling is legal in Estonia. Estonia's gambling market is well-regulated. The body that oversees gambling in Estonia is the Tax and Customs Board. 

Before operating in Estonia, casino operators must get a license from the above. This license requires them to adhere to specific regulations that protect players. One of the regulations is player protection. Casinos may do this in several ways, including providing responsible gambling tools. These tools are to prevent gambling addiction and help those who struggle with it. Player protection also involves protecting the player's financial and personal information.

Only operators with licenses can offer gambling services to Estonians. The Estonian Tax and Customs Board has a list of licensed operators on its website. This list helps Estonians decide which website practices transparency and protection.

Years before it became legal, gambling was not allowed in Estonia. The country was part of the USSR, and gambling was illegal across territories. It was after independence that the Estonian government legalized gambling. The revenue they would make from taxing the industry made them give in. They saw the importance of the online gaming industry in rejuvenating the economy. So, in 1994, the government enacted the Lottery Act and the Gambling Act in 1996. 

When the government enacted them, the Acts focused on land-based casinos. But when the online gambling trend reached Estonia, the government reacted immediately. They amended the Gambling Act in 2010 to include online gambling activities. They also legalized it and made laws to regulate it so casino players would be safe. The regulations and acceptance of online gambling have helped the Estonian economy survive.

Gambling Act and the Lottery Act

The online gambling regulations in Estonia are the Gambling Act and the Lottery Act. Both are primary legislation that is important to the gambling industry in Estonia. Below is a brief overview of both Acts:

Gambling Act

The Act came into play in 2009, legalizing and regulating all forms of gambling. It was later amended in 2010 to legalize and regulate online gambling. The Act requires operators to get a license from the Estonian Tax and Customs Board. It divides the gambling licenses based on the types of gambling the casino offers.

Apart from licensing, the Gambling Act talked about regulations. It lays down rules that gambling operators must follow. Apart from regulating, these rules will help them in fair play and player protection. It will also help the operators protect casinos from criminal activities. Some of the regulations include the integrity of games and transparent operations. The Act requires operators to use player protection measures to safeguard Estonian players. Some of these are age verification, self-exclusion options, and deposit limits.

Finally, the Act provides for the taxation of casino operators. This is one way to ensure they contribute to state funds and improve society. Both the players and operators pay taxes to the government. The Act prohibits casinos from making exaggerated advertisements. It also prohibits them from targeting vulnerable demographics like minors in their advertisement.

Lottery Act

The Estonian Lottery Act only regulated Lotteries. The government created it in 1994, before the Gambling Act. Like the Gambling Act, it requires lottery operators to get licensed by the Tax and Customs Board. The Act aims to ensure that lotteries are fair and transparent to players. 

The Lottery Act divided lotteries into three types: traditional, instant, and electronic. Each of these lotteries has separate regulations guiding them in the Act. To keep the activities connected to the growth of society. The Act provides that some percentage of Lottery revenue must go towards a social cause. This cause may be cultural, educational, or charity purposes. 

The Act mandates Lotteries to stick to healthy practices and protect their players. They practice age restriction and provide responsible gambling tools. Lotteries that advertise must not encourage excess gambling that puts players at risk.

The Tax and Customs Board enforces the Gambling Act and the Lottery Act. The board ensures that casinos and lotteries follow the regulations.

Estonian Legal Gambling Age

The legal age, particularly in online and land-based gambling in Estonia, is 21 years old.

Estonian Tax and Customs Board

The Tax and Customs Board regulates all forms of gambling and lotteries in Estonia. It must inspect and track casino activities and ensure the safety of players. The board's duty extends to both online and land-based gambling.

The Tax and Customs Board issues two types of licenses: the Activity and Operating Licenses. The Activity license confirms that the company is reliable to play in. The operating license deals with the games that the casino may offer. The board taxes casino operators based on their net profit, and they only pay 5%. The players have a tax percentage of 18% on their lottery winnings. Players are not taxed on their winnings from Casinos and Sportsbooks.

The Tax and Customs Board gives the license to operators ready to pay the tax and license fee. The board places other casino sites that do not have the valid license on a blacklist. Players can find the blocklist on the official board website. They also have a list of operators who have received a valid license. The healthy practice and loose legislation for gambling have made Estonian players safe. Players do not risk fraudulent casinos to enjoy gaming because the law has given options.
