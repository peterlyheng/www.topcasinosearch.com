Online Gambling Legality in Germany

Since 2021 and the introduction of Glücksspielstaatsvertrag, Germany has been pushing hard to create a unified gambling market consisting only of online gaming platforms with a valid license from a German regulatory body.

According to evz.de, casinos licensed outside Germany (which also includes casinos regulated by other EU members, such as Malta) are not considered legal anymore. In fact, the popular site claims that playing on non-German licensed casinos is considered a crime.

We have no information on players being prosecuted for playing in offshore licensed casinos. However, if you want to join a site licensed by popular non-German regulators (including those from Malta or Curacao), it’s best to consult a legal professional for more information.

Yes, online casinos are legal in Germany. However, it's important to note that the regulations and licensing requirements have recently undergone changes. Can I use Euros in German casinos?

Yes, you can use Euros (€) as the primary monetary unit in German online casinos. Since Germany is part of the Eurozone, the Euro is widely accepted, making it convenient for players to deposit, wager, and withdraw funds in their local currency.
