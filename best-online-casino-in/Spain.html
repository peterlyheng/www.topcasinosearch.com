Online Gambling Laws and Regulations in Spain

Online gambling rules in Spain have seen major legal fights and rule changes since the Spanish Royal Decree 958/2020. This decree was enacted in November 2020. The decree aimed to limit how much minors see gambling ads. The primary approach was to ban sponsorships and celebrity endorsements, but it ran into legal problems. The Spanish Supreme Court decided in November 2023 to cancel some parts of the decree.

Here are the important aspects of the Spanish online gambling laws:

  1. Royal Decree 176/2023: This new law makes gambling safer by limiting how gambling is advertised. It regulates how long players can gamble in one session.
  2. Advertising Restrictions: Since 2020, strict limits have been placed on gambling ads. This measure was taken to prevent people from gambling too much.
  3. Player Protection: The law helps players by identifying those who gamble a lot, especially young people. It gives them particular messages and limits on offers to help them gamble responsibly.
  4. Self-Exclusion: Players can register with a special list to stop gambling for a period. During this time, they won't get ads or be able to gamble.
  5. Responsibility Messages: Gambling websites must display messages about responsible gambling while people play. These messages may differ depending on how old players are or how they gamble.
    Government Oversight: Gambling operators must report annually on their protocols for managing risky behaviors. This encourages enhancing transparency and accountability in the industry.

Does the Spanish Gambling Law Tax Online Casino Winnings?

Yes, the Spanish Gambling Law does tax online casino winnings. Any income earned from gambling, including online casino winnings, is subject to taxation in Spain. The tax rate varies based on the amount of the winnings and the type of gambling activity.

Money won from online casino games or sports bets must be reported as capital gains in your Personal Income Tax (IRPF) return.

The exact percentage of tax you'll pay falls between 19% and 47%, depending on how much you've won. If your yearly income doesn't exceed 23,000 euros and your prize isn't over 5,000 euros, you might not need to declare it.

Certain prizes higher than this limit, like those from raffles, poker tournaments, sports betting, or other games of chance, are subject to income tax. If the prize you win isn't money – like a car or a trip – you're responsible for paying taxes on the value of that gift.

If you face losses from online casino games, you can use these losses to reduce the amount of tax you owe on your winnings or prizes. But remember, these deductions can't exceed the amount you've won.

History of Online Gambling in Spain

Early Years and Lack of Regulation:

  • In the late 1990s and early 2000s, online casinos started gaining popularity in Spain, as they did worldwide.
  • During this period, there was a lack of proper regulation for online betting activities. Spanish players could access international gambling sites without specific restrictions.

Emergence of Regulatory Framework:

  • Realizing the potential revenue and the need to protect consumers, the Spanish government began to consider regulating online betting.
  • In 2010, the Spanish Gambling Act was passed, establishing a legal framework for online betting activities.
  • The act allowed for the issuance of licenses to online casino operators, making it legal for them to offer their services to Spanish players.

Licensing and Regulation:

  • In 2012, the Spanish regulatory authority, Dirección General de Ordenación del Juego (DGOJ), started granting licenses to online casino operators who met the required standards.
  • This marked the official beginning of regulated online betting in Spain. Licensed operators could offer various games, including sports betting, casino games, poker, and more. By 2014, they were also allowed to offer slot machines to their players.

Player Protection and Responsible Gaming:

  • One of the key aims of the regulatory framework was to ensure player protection and responsible gaming practices.
  • Licensed operators were required to adhere to strict rules. These included player age verification, responsible advertising, and measures to prevent problem gambling.

Shared Liquidity and Growth:

  • In 2018, Spain joined the shared liquidity agreement with France, Italy, and Portugal for online poker. This allowed players from these countries to play against each other, increasing player and prize pools.
  • The regulated online gambling market continued to grow. There was an increase in the number of legal online casinos in Spain and games available to Spanish players.
  • The online gambling market grew, with more legal casinos and games for Spanish players.

Recent Developments: 

In recent years, Spain has made more rules to keep gambling safe:

  • Royal Decree 985/2020: Stricter rules on gambling ads to protect players.
  • Royal Decree 176/2023: New rules from September 2023 to make gambling safer. These include limits on how long players can gamble, identifying players who bet too much, and special protections for young players under 25.
  • Self-Exclusion: Players can choose to take a break from gambling and won't receive ads during this time.
  • Responsible Gambling Messages: Gambling websites must show messages to remind players to gamble responsibly.


Spain continues to improve its gambling laws to protect players and keep the industry safe. Overall, the country has embraced online betting as a revenue source. It also places a high priority on player protection and responsible gaming practices.
