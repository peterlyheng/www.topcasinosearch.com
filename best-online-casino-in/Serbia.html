Gambling Laws in Serbia

Legal Gambling in Serbia

Gambling in Serbia is regulated by the the Games of Chance Administration in accordance with the Law on Games of Chance.

Online Gambling Laws for Gambling in Serbia

Online gambling in Serbia is legal under the Gambling Law, but here it gets tricky.

According to Article 10 of the Law on Games of Chance, it is forbidden to "participate in games of chance organized abroad, for which the stakes were placed within the territory of the Republic of Serbia."

That means that Serbian players are required to gamble at native online and land-based operators. To play games at offshore casino sites seems not to be permitted.

However, the enforcement of these regulations is reportedly not strict. In fact, the law does not seem to prescribe penalties for Serbian players that play offshore online casinos. Moreover, the paragraph on online gambling is somewhat vague.

In 2012, the Serbian Gaming Board issued a directive to block unlicensed gambling sites, among which were some of the top-rated online casinos, which would have been a regulatory measure aimed at curbing unauthorized online gambling activities and ensuring compliance with national gambling laws.

The bottom line: While playing at foreign online casinos is not strictly regulated, there are no registered penalties or imprisonment for those who opt for this route.

Land-Based Casinos in Serbia Regulation

As per the Games of Chance Act, Serbia allows ten casinos to operate in the gambling market across its regions.

Currently, only two Belgrade-based casinos are licensed. Both Grand Casino Beograd and Fair Play Casino are located within large hotels. They provide various gaming options, including blackjack, roulette and poker tables, as well as a diverse selection of slot machines. Notably, the Grand Casino Beograd hosts the biannual Danube Poker Masters Series.

Taxation on Gambling Winnings in Serbia

Serbia doesn't tax winnings stemming from the gambling activities.

Regulation and Oversight

The cornerstone of gambling regulation in Serbia is the Games of Chance Act of 2011. This Act ended the state monopoly in online gambling and introduced a more diverse gambling landscape. The new law entered into force in 2020.

Serbian Gaming Board

Established by the Law on Games of Chance in Serbia in 2011, Uprava za igre na sreću, the Games of Chance Administration, a Serbian Ministry of Finance department, oversees all aspects of offline and online gambling in Serbia, including licensing, regulations, monitoring, and taxation.

On March 1, 2019, this body officially began to oversee and regulate casino games within the country's gambling market. The administration is headquartered in Belgrade. Its funding comes from the public budget.

In 2020, a new Law on Games of Chance took effect, granting the administration independent authority to regulate gambling games across the country.

History of Gambling in Serbia

Gambling has a storied history in Serbia, with land-based casinos dating back to the 1960s. The Yugoslavian Wars and the subsequent separation of Serbia from Yugoslavia in 1992 led to audits of many land-based casinos. In 2004, the Games of Chance Act was introduced, legalizing various forms of gambling, including classic and special casino games, prize competitions, and internet gambling.

In 2011, significant changes occurred with eliminating the National Lottery's monopoly, allowing for more diverse gambling options. This change also brought restrictions on offshore casinos for Serbian players, emphasizing the importance of playing at licensed and approved native Serbia online casino sites.
