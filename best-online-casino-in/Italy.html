Is Online Gambling Legal in Italy?

Italy's gambling history, reaching back to the Roman Empire, took a new turn in 2011 when the government made gambling legal. This change allowed local operators to start online casinos, making gambling more accessible. The AAMS (Amministrazione Autonoma dei Monopoli di Stato – Autonomous Administration of State Monopolies) is the Italian gaming authority. It was established in 2006 and is the body responsible for issuing licenses in Italy. Following a 2009 EU order, Italy declared foreign online casinos legal.

The Italian gambling license is for both local and foreign online gambling operators. This involves a €300,000 fee, strict requirements, and careful reviews by AAMS. With over 160 active licenses issued, online casinos in Italy are entirely legal. The well-regulated market emphasizes using licensed platforms. As rules in the online casino gambling world keep changing, players must stay informed for a legal and enjoyable gaming experience in Italy.

What is the Legal Gambling Age in Italy?

You need to be 18 years of age to gamble in Italy legally. Whether at a casino, sports betting shop, or online, this rule is in place to ensure gambling is responsible and follows the law.

Do I Need to Pay Taxes on Online Gambling Winnings in Italy?

Yes, you do need to pay taxes on your winnings at online casinos in Italy. Previously, winnings over €500 were taxed at 6%, which is set to increase to 10% later. Lottery winnings over €500 faced an 8% tax. Italian winners must declare their gambling income for taxes. If you play from outside Italy, you might pay little to no taxes on your winnings.
