History of Gambling in Slovenia

Pre-Regulation Era: Online gambling in Slovenia existed before the government implemented regulations. Slovenian players could access international online casinos without any specific local laws governing the activity.

2013: Slovenia introduced its first comprehensive gambling legislation, the "Gambling Act," which aimed to regulate all forms of gambling, including online gambling. The Act outlined requirements for operators and established a licensing system.

2015: The first online gambling licenses were issued in Slovenia. The new regulations allowed domestic operators to offer online casino games and sports betting services. However, foreign operators still faced restrictions.

2017: The European Commission (EC) expressed concerns about the restrictions on foreign operators. The Commission stated that Slovenia's regulations did not comply with EU law on the free movement of services.

2019: Slovenia faced pressure from the EC to amend its gambling act to align with EU regulations. The country was given a deadline to do so.

2020: In response to pressure from the EC, Slovenia proposed amendments to its gambling laws in early 2020—the amendments aimed to liberalize the online gambling Slovenian market by allowing foreign operators to apply for licenses.

2021: Towards the end of 2021, a draft of a new Gambling Act was presented. If this new legislation is approved, it could open the doors for foreign operators to enter the Slovenian local market.
