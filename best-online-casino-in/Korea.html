    Online Gambling Laws and Regulations in Korea

    South Korea is known for its advanced technology in East Asia. However, online gambling sites are strictly prohibited within its borders. As a result, citizens turn to licensed operators in other countries for online gambling services.

    Historically, gambling has been part of Korean culture. This includes betting on native board games and sports. However, strict laws were introduced after the creation of the Republic of Korea. The government banned gambling within the country and even at foreign casinos under the 'Habitual Overseas Gambler' law.

    While physical gambling laws have eased over time, online gambling remains illegal in South Korea. Companies and individuals cannot run online casinos within the country. Despite these restrictions, South Koreans access bookmakers and casino sites like Ladbrokes and 888 overseas. To bypass government blocks on these sites, players use e-wallets and VPNs. Some companies in South Korea also operate illegal online sports betting and casinos. The unlawful gambling market was estimated at US$66 billion in 2012, leading to police crackdowns on these activities.

    One exception to the strict laws is betting on horseracing, which was legalized early on. South Korea also allows betting on boat racing, cycling, and a national lottery. Land-based casinos were illegal until 1967, when they were allowed in large hotels for foreign guests. The only land casino for Korean citizens, Kangwon Land Casino, opened in 2000 and is very popular.
