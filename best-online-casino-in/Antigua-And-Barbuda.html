Is Gambling Legal in Antigua and Barbuda?

Yes, gambling has been LEGAL in Antigua and Barbuda since 1994.

Is Online Gambling Legal in Antigua and Barbuda?

Yes, online gambling is LEGAL in Antigua and Barbuda.

Though legal, very few companies have licenses. The reason is the collapse of the gambling market after the conflict with the U.S.

Who Can Accept Players from Antigua and Barbuda?

Any casino holding a license from the Directorate of Offshore Gambling.

To accept players, both land-based and online casinos must obtain interactive gambling licenses.

Can Tourists Gamble Online from Antigua and Barbuda?

The laws apply to lawful residents and citizens. Tourists and other temporary residents can play at Antigua and Barbuda casinos.

Who Regulates Gambling in Antigua and Barbuda?

The Financial Services Regulatory Commission (FSRC) oversees the offshore gaming industry in A&B. 

Its Directorate of Offshore Gaming regulates the market and handles licensing. The Division of Gaming is a regulatory body under its wing.

What Types of Casino Licenses Exist in Antigua and Barbuda?

There are two types of gambling licenses:

  1. Interactive Wagering (totalizer license)
  2. Interactive Gambling (online casino license)

The former applies to betting activities. The latter to casino games, lotteries, keno and bingo.

Every online casino company must establish a physical presence to apply for the licenses.

What is the Legal Gambling Age in Antigua and Barbuda?

Players from Antigua and Barbuda must turn 18 before they can legally gamble.

Are There Any Land-Based Casinos in Antigua and Barbuda?

Yes, a few. Currently, five casinos are operating. All of them you'll find in Antigua. These venues welcome tourists and locals alike.

Where Are the Biggest and Most Popular Land-Based Casinos in Antigua and Barbuda?

Ayre Resort & Casino is Antigua's biggest gambling house and the first 5-star casino. The luxurious venue opened in 2023. The controversial owner claimed to have financed the casino with his Bitcoin earnings.

The biggest venue used to be King’s Casino in St. John’s. King's features the world's most giant slot machine.

Is There a Gambling Tax in Antigua and Barbuda?

Yes. Both brick-and-mortar and online casinos are subject to taxation.

Do I Have to Pay Taxes on Gambling Winnings in Antigua and Barbuda?

Good news. Gambling winnings in Antigua and Barbuda are NOT TAXABLE.

Is Crypto Gambling Regulated in Antigua and Barbuda?

Crypto gambling is NOT REGULATED. In fact, the relevant laws seem to favor any alternatives referred to as "tokens."

The law states that casinos are to accept any method approved by the regulatory body. But on the matter of cryptocurrencies, the Directorate seems not to say anything.

If whatever is not explicitly allowed is illegal, then casinos shouldn't accept cryptocurrencies. If what is not forbidden is permitted, then we're dealing with an entirely different story.
