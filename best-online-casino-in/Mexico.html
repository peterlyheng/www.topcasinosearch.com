Gambling Laws and Regulations in Mexico

Mexico's online gambling industry operates under the oversight of SEGOB (Secretaría de Gobernación) and international licenses. These provide strict online gambling laws such as the Gaming Law and the Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Law. They ensure fairness and transparency and prevent illegal activities like money laundering. These laws also define operator responsibilities, advertising rules, enforcement procedures, and tax obligations. Additionally, organizations like GambleAware and GamCare promote responsible gambling practices.

Critical aspects of Mexico's online gambling legislation:

Regulatory Agencies:

  • Government bodies like the Ministry of Finance oversee Mexico's gambling industry. 
  • They enforce rules through special agencies. The FIU checks for money laundering, and the Deputy General Directorate of Legal Processes handles licensing and legal issues.

Licensing Requirements:

  • To operate legally, gambling operators in Mexico must register and verify the identities of their clients using official documents. 
  • They also need to know who benefits from the gambling transactions. These rules help prevent illegal money activities.

Advertising Regulations:

  • Ads for gambling in Mexico must be clear and truthful, not promoting betting too strongly. 
  • They must include the operator's license number and warn against underage gambling. 
  • These rules protect consumers and ensure fair advertising.

Enforcement and Sanctions:

  • Regulators in Mexico have the power to punish gambling operators who break the rules. 
  • Penalties can include fines or the closure of businesses. 
  • These measures aim to keep the industry honest and responsible.

Taxation:

  • The government taxes gambling in Mexico to fund public services. 
  • Operators pay federal taxes based on revenue and must withhold taxes on winnings. 
  • Some states may also have additional taxes for gambling.

Gambling History in Mexico

The history of gambling in Mexico is a tale of transformation and regulation. Despite its rich cultural background, gambling faced legal hurdles for decades. In 1935, Mexico declared all games of chance illegal, putting a halt to various forms of betting, from cockfighting to lotteries.

The landscape shifted dramatically in 2014 when the government embraced regulation after 70 years of stagnation. Land-based casinos began to thrive in nearly every state, save for Oaxaca, Tlaxcala, and Zacatecas. Additionally, virtual casinos gained popularity, further expanding gaming options.

Significant milestones mark the country's gambling history:

The loosening of restrictions in the 2000s led to the establishment of new casinos across Mexico, contributing to the industry's growth. Though not all forms of gambling have been legalized, the country's approach to its laws reflects a degree of tolerance. Land-based casinos, sports betting, and even controversial activities like cockfighting and bullfighting gained prominence.

The online gambling sector underwent its evolution. With the rise of the internet in the late 1990s, many online casinos emerged, facing initial challenges due to regulatory gaps. Operators like Jackpot City, Casino Época, and Spin Palace entered the scene during this period. In 2004, Mexico reformed its Law of Games and Raffles, though it wasn't until a decade later that online gambling sites gained legal status under the Ministry of the Interior's guidance.

Is Online Gambling Legal in Mexico, and Who Regulates It?

Online gambling in Mexico operates under regulations established by the government. The responsible is the Ministry of Interior (Secretaría de Gobernación, “SEGOB”), through its Gambling and Raffles Bureau (Dirección General de Juegos y Sorteos), also known as the “Bureau”.

The Federal Gaming and Sweepstakes Act of 1947 governs gambling, while the comprehensive regulations legislative package dates from 2004.

As of 2014, the SEGOB oversees online gambling activities in the country, ensuring fair play and responsible gambling. While casinos approved by this authority are preferred, Mexican players can also explore reputable international casinos.
