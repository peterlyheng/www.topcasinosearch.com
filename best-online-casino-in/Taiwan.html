Wagering Requirements

A wagering requirement is also called a playthrough requirement. It is the number of times that the bonus amount must have been wagered before a player can receive the bonus. For example, if a bonus is $10 with a 3x requirement, the player must have wagered $300 before they can withdraw winnings from the bonus.

Each casino game contributes to wager requirements at different levels. While a Slots game may contribute 100% of the wagering requirement, a Poker game may contribute only 50%. Another important fact is that wagering requirements have time limits to be achieved. Wagering requirements vary based on each bonus and casino. We advise that you read them carefully before attempting to get a bonus. The best online casinos in Taiwan usually have a 25x-50x playthrough requirement for bonus-associated withdrawals. For non-bonus withdrawals, a 1x requirement is fair.

Online Gambling Laws and Regulations in Taiwan

The laws that regulate online casinos in Taiwan are ambiguous about its validity. However, some laws have implied that Online gambling is illegal in Taiwan. Laws like the Criminal Code and the Offshore Islands Development Act make gambling activities prohibited, including online gambling.

Are Online Casinos Legal in Taiwan?

The Criminal Code Act prohibits gambling activities both online and offline. Section 266 specifically provides penalties for discovered defaulters. Also, the Offshore Islands Development Act imposes extra restrictions on gambling in certain areas and locations in Taiwan, including Penghu, Kinmen, and Matsu. 

Despite the laws making gambling activities illegal, there are exceptions. The Taiwan Sports Lottery Corporation runs state-run lotteries and sports betting under certain conditions. Social gambling among friends in private gatherings is also not prohibited by the law. This means that online casinos are not legal, and the government remains on watch for persons who illegally engage in online gambling.

How Does Taxation Work in Taiwan

Gambling taxation in Taiwan only applies to state-run lotteries and specific licensed gambling activities. The Taiwan government operates the Taiwan lottery and the Uniform Invoice lottery. The revenue made by these gambling agencies is donated to charities and for public benefit, so it is not subject to tax. Gamblers' bet winnings are also not subject to taxation of any kind. 

The government licenses certain gambling activities, such as horse racing and sports betting. The government taxes these gambling houses based on their revenue, the type of gambling services they offer, and the specifics of their income. Bet winnings are generally not subject to personal income tax by the government.
