The Laws About Gambling in Albania

In 2018, Albania banned gambling. The governing Socialist Party made this decision because the industry had grown very large, and many businesses were linked to criminal groups. The ban started on January 1, 2019, and only one casino was allowed to operate after that.

On February 15, 2024, the Albanian Parliament passed a new law, No. 18/2024, changing the rules about gambling. This law ended the five-year ban on sports betting, starting from March 27, 2024.

Key points of the new Gambling Law include:

  • Sports Betting will be allowed only online with licenses from the Gaming Authority.
  • Only ten online sports betting licenses will be given through a competitive process.
  • During the license period, a license fee of at least 400,000,000 ALL (about EUR 3.9 million) must be paid in equal parts.

Who Can Apply for a License:

  • Companies registered as joint-stock companies in Albania with at least 40 million ALL (about EUR 400,000) in share capital.
  • Companies with at least three years of gambling experience in three EU/OECD countries, either directly or through a shareholder, owning at least 30% of the company’s shares.
  • Companies with a turnover of at least 2 billion ALL (about EUR 19.8 million) from gambling in the last financial year.
  • Companies with the necessary administrative and organizational skills.
  • Companies with an agreement for the necessary programs, systems, websites, and applications for online sports betting.

Learn the History of Gambling in Albania

The history of gambling in Albania is quite turbulent, reflecting the country's broader political and economic shifts. After being strictly prohibited during the communist era, the industry saw a boom with the country's move to a market economy. However, this boom also brought issues such as gambling addiction and criminal activity, leading to increased regulation in the 2000s and 2010s. The current strict ban reflects the government's ongoing concern about these issues, although it's unclear how long this policy will remain in effect and how it will change in the future.

  1. Before 1991: Albania was under a communist regime led by Enver Hoxha from the end of World War II until 1991. During this period, all forms of gambling were strictly prohibited, reflecting the government's stance on prohibiting private enterprise and maintaining state control over all aspects of life.
  2. 1991 - 2005: With the fall of communism in 1991, Albania transitioned towards a free-market economy. This period saw a gradual liberalization of various sectors, including gambling. Many casinos and other gambling establishments opened during this time, mainly catering to tourists and a growing middle class. However, there was little regulation in place to manage the burgeoning industry.
  3. 2005 - 2012: In the mid-2000s, the Albanian government began implementing regulations to oversee the gambling industry, aiming to combat associated illegal activities and problem gambling. This included licensing requirements for operators and the introduction of some player protection measures.
  4. 2012 - 2018: The government, noting the increasing issues related to problem gambling and organized crime, passed stricter laws regulating the gambling industry. In 2013, it decided to ban all forms of online gambling. However, the enforcement was inconsistent and the law was somewhat relaxed over time, allowing some forms of online sports betting.
  5. 2019 - Present: In 2019, the government implemented a comprehensive ban on all gambling, with the exception of the state-run lottery and on-premise gambling at major hotels. This decision aimed to combat the increase in problem gambling, particularly among young people, and the connection between the gambling industry and organized crime. The implementation of this ban led to the closure of all gambling shops, casinos, and sports betting venues in residential areas.
  6. 2024: On February 15, 2024, the Albanian Parliament passed a new law, No. 18/2024. This law made gambling legal in the country and changed the rules about gambling. Today, gambling is popular in Albania, and the government uses the money it makes to help the country.
