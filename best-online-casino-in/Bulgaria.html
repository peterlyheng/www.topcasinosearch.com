The legal status of online gambling within Bulgarian borders

Online gambling is completely legal in Bulgaria. Compared to some other European countries, Bulgarian online gambling laws are not harsh. Having said that, only legal casinos are allowed to operate online casinos in Bulgaria.

Specific laws and regulations are in place to ensure a safe and fair gaming environment in Bulgaria online casinos. These regulations are overseen by the Bulgarian State Commission on Gambling (SCG). It handles issuing licenses to online casino operators.

To operate legally in Bulgaria, online casino operators must get a license from the SCG. This license proves that the casino meets specific standards and requirements of the Bulgarian gambling law. It ensures the casino operates transparently, protects players' rights, and upholds responsible gambling practices.

The licensing process involves thorough scrutiny of the casino's operations. This includes financial stability, technical infrastructure, and adherence to anti-money laundering regulations. Once a license is granted, the casino is subject to ongoing monitoring to ensure compliance with online gambling laws.

Bulgarian gambling law also requires that online casinos use approved software. This certification ensures that the games are fair and unbiased, giving players a genuine chance of winning.

Online Gambling History in Bulgaria

The history of online gambling in Bulgaria has shown changes over time. In the early 1990s, as Bulgaria turned democratic, gambling began. In 1993, the Law on Gambling Games made the rules for casinos and gambling.

Around the mid-2000s, online gambling worldwide became famous. Bulgaria noticed and created the Gambling Act in 2012. It controlled both online and land-based gambling. The law stated the need for licenses for online gambling in Bulgaria.

In 2013, amendments to the law brought about tax reductions. From that point, the best online casinos in Bulgaria were required to pay 20% of their earnings to the country's budget with a licensing fee of €50,000.

In 2014, Bulgaria started giving licenses for online gambling. Operators could legally offer online gambling. This aimed to earn money for the budget and make gambling safer.

Still, there were problems at the start. The regulatory framework faced criticisms for its strict requirements, and some operators struggled to meet them. This led to a relatively low number of licensed online gambling operators in the early stages.

By 2019, the Gambling Act was amended. It aimed to make Bulgaria's online gambling better and safer.

This regulatory change had a positive impact on both players and the nation. Presently, any Bulgarian player can discover an authorized and secure online casino. At the same time, the government is reaping large rewards in the form of gambling tax revenue. Just a few years ago, the country was generating over $80 million from online casinos. This number increases each year with the emergence of new online casinos within the Bulgarian market.
