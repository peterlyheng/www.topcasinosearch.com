Is Online Gambling Legal in Guatemala?

Gambling rules in Guatemala are a bit complicated. Back in 1880, all types of gambling, even games of chance, were said to be illegal. But in 1956, gambling facilities got permission to open under specific conditions. The Decreto Número 574 El Presidente De La República granted permission, provided they obtained a license from the Ministry of Interior. The Ministry gives licenses and takes fees but doesn't actively check if the places are doing anything illegal.

Even though land-based activities like the lottery are legal, there's no official list, so it's hard to keep track of how the gaming industry is doing. Online gambling started in 2008, but no laws are regulating it. In 2013, there was a suggestion to report transactions over $10,000 and tax people involved in online gambling, but this didn't become law.

To sum up, it's not clear if online gambling is legal in Guatemala because there are no specific laws. Still, people from Guatemala can gamble online as offshore providers accept them.

What is the Guatemalan Legal gambling age?

The legal gambling age in Guatemala is 18 years old. However, online gambling is not regulated, so players must make an effort to keep themselves safe.

Are taxes Collected on Online casino winnings in Guatemala?

Because there are no specific laws regulating online gambling in Guatemala, there is no taxation system for online casinos. You do not have to pay taxes on your winnings. However, we recommend talking to a legal tax adviser if you get a huge cash out.

History of Online Gambling in Guatemala

Surprisingly, Guatemala is one of the first Central American countries to regulate games of chance. Way back in 1880, the Guatemala Penal Code said gambling was illegal. But things changed in 1956 when the President allowed gaming facilities to open if they got a license from the Ministry of Interior. However, this system had weak spots and loopholes.

The Ministry of Interior gives licenses and collects fees but doesn't actively check if places are doing anything wrong, like money laundering. Without an official list of licensed places, it's hard to know how the gambling industry is doing. During COVID-19, fewer tourists visited Central America, suggesting the land-based gambling sector faced losses, but it's unclear how much.

Although the lottery is the only officially legal form of gambling, run by a private company, Supreme Ventures Ltd, land-based activities became legal in 2005, and online gambling started in 2008. Sports betting is not officially legal but could get licenses from the Ministry of Interior, making it kind of legal.

Despite efforts to regulate online gambling, nothing specific has become law. In 2013, there was a suggestion to report big transactions and tax people involved in online gambling, but it didn't become law. There was also an idea to create an agency to watch over the industry, but nothing happened. Even without clear laws, people in Guatemala still gamble online because offshore providers accept them, letting them place bets and get bonuses.
