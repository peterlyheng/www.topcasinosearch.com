Puerto Rican Gambling Laws

Online gambling is legal in Puerto Rico. In 2019, The government passed a bill called House Resolution 2038. Governor Wanda Vázquez Garced signed this bill. The bill legalized land-based gambling, online sports betting, and online casinos. While permitting to play online casinos legally, the bill did not specify how casinos should operate. Thus leaving the country in a legal gray area. As players, the most important thing to know is that you can legally play online casinos in Puerto Rico.

While the law doesn't state what are the regulatory requirements. And there are no online casinos with permits from Puerto Rico specifically. Players can play off-shore brands freely.

As a part of the bill, the Puerto Rico Gaming Commission has been established. Its primary job is to develop gambling regulations in the country. It is not clear when new laws will pass and what their contents will be. For now, gamble away.

History of Gambling in Puerto Rico

Gambling has deep roots in Puerto Rico, going back even before Spanish colonization in the 15th century. Information about what were the popular gambling activities back then is hard to find. 

The Spanish brought some of the oldest gambling traditions, like cockfighting. And while cockfighting is not allowed in any US territory, it is considered a cultural activity in Puerto Rico. Horse racing, another popular gambling form on the island, became regulated in the early 1900s. Casinos were officially legalized in 1948, with the first opening a year later, and now there are over 16 casinos across the island. 

Other key moments include:

  • The start of the Puerto Rico Lottery in 1934.
  • The establishment of the Puerto Rico Tourism Company in 1970.
  • A 1989 ruling that gave Puerto Rico complete control over its gambling.

Gambling is a significant part of the culture and economy, both for locals and as a tourist attraction. It's a substantial money-maker for the government and an exciting part of life in Puerto Rico that's here to stay.
