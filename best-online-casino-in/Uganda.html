Online Gambling Laws and Regulations in Uganda

Online gambling has become popular in Uganda, allowing people to enjoy casino games from home or on their mobile devices. But it's essential to understand the rules that oversee this in Uganda. Keep reading this section for everything you need to know about the online gambling laws in Uganda.

Are Online Casinos Legal in Uganda?

Online gambling in Uganda exists in a bit of a legal gray area. The primary law controlling gambling in Uganda is the Lotteries and Gaming Act of 1967. It was made long before the Internet, so it doesn't directly address online gambling.

While it's not illegal, the government is working to regulate and control all gambling, whether online or not.

Lotteries and Gaming Regulatory Board Uganda (LGBR) is tasked with regulating and managing all online gambling activities in the country.

The Lotteries and Gaming Bill in 2013

In 2013, Uganda introduced the Lotteries and Gaming Bill, a law meant to update and modernize the country's gambling rules. It was made to handle new challenges in the gambling world, including online gambling. This law established a lottery and gaming fund. It also provides licenses, regulates lotteries and gaming, and taxes casinos and gaming operations. It repealed the National Lotteries Act and the Gaming and Pool Betting Act.

What is the Legal online gambling age in Uganda?

The minimum age for gambling is 25 years old in Uganda. This means you must be at least 25 to gamble, whether at a physical casino or online.

Does Uganda collect taxes on Online casino winnings?

Yes, Uganda taxes online casino winnings. A 15% tax is imposed on all forms of gambling earnings. The casino operators usually withhold this tax and then remit the taxes to the authorities.

History of Online Gambling in Uganda

The history of online gambling in Uganda has seen changes due to the Internet's arrival in the late 1990s and early 2000s. Here's a brief outlook:

  • Uganda's main gambling law, dating back to 1967, didn't consider online gambling because it was made long before the Internet.
  • With the arrival of the Internet in the late 1990s to early 2000s, some casino sites started offering their services to Ugandans. Then, people could play casino games and bet using their computers.
  • The challenge remained. There were no specific rules for online gambling, making it a bit confusing for everyone. The government tried to control gambling, including online, but it wasn't easy due to the evolving nature of the industry.
  • In 2013, Uganda introduced a bill to update gambling rules, including online gambling. This showed that the government wanted to keep up with changes in gambling.
  • The government of Uganda has passed some bills since then to regulate the online gambling industry. The most recent being the Lotteries and Gaming (Amendment) Bill, 2023. It was passed to revise the existing tax on Ugandan casinos from 20% to 30% per gaming activity.
