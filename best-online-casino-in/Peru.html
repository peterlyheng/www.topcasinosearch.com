Peruvian Online Gambling Law and Regulations

Peru, known for its rich history, culture, and breathtaking landscapes, is home to a thriving online casino gaming scene. But are online casinos legal in Peru? At what age can you start gambling online? Do you need to pay taxes on gambling wins? Let's explore the law that governs online betting in this diverse nation.

Online Casinos Legality in Peru

Peru, unlike many other countries, has embraced the online gambling industry. The government recognizes the potential benefits it can bring, such as increased tourism and economic growth. This has led to a regulated and legal online betting market. Thus offering players a safe and secure environment to enjoy their favorite games.

Peru has made significant progress in online betting regulations. The Ministry of Foreign Trade and Tourism introduced laws to oversee these activities. On October 13, 2023, the government published new laws for gambling online. These regulations are outlined in Supreme Decree Nº 005-2023 and build upon Law No. 31557, which was later modified by Law No. 31806 to introduce a licensing system for online gambling in Peru. It also imposed a 12% tax on profits, expected to generate about $40 million annually. Starting in April 2024, licensed entities will need to pay a 12% tax on their net profits. This new law marks Peru's official entry into the regulated online betting arena.

To operate legally in Peru, online casinos must obtain a license from the regulatory authority. This is the General Directorate of Casino Games and Slot Machines (DGJCMT). This body ensures that casinos adhere to strict regulations: fair gaming practices and responsible gambling measures.

So, the answer to the question is clear: yes, online casinos are legal in Peru. Players can enjoy a broad range of options, from slots to table games, all within the confines of the law.
