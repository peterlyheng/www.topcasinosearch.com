Online Gambling Laws and Regulations in United States

The legality of online casinos in the US differs from one state to another. In some states, you can gamble on a selected few websites. In other states, you can't gamble at all. This situation wasn't always the same. Before 2006 you could gamble freely because there were no clear rules regarding this subject. Back in 2006, the UIGEA act was issued. Since then, many gambling companies have left the US and never returned to accept US players. The reason was that this act made it extremely hard to accept US players legally to online casinos. In 2012, another set of rules were set that enabled each state to make their regulations regarding gambling. Since then, more companies have joined the market, and now more brands can accept players from the US. The situation isn't optimal for players because many online casinos still do not accept convectional payment methods such as credit cards and wire transfers. It is hard to find an easy way to deposit the leasing brands. And this has made the cryptocurrency payment option a trendy one for US players. In any way, the rules don't matter too much to you as a player. It only makes it harder to make deposits. In case of a prosecution, the government will go after the online casino brands and not the players. There hasn't been any lawsuit against a private player in the US. So, if you find an online casino you like, which accepts your funds one way or another, you can play freely.
