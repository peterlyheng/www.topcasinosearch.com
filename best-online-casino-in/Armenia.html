Current Laws Regarding Online Gambling in Armenia

Gambling is well integrated in Armenian culture. In the years before, the government has had a liberal stance on online gambling. Although this allowed players to flourish at foreign casinos, it had its defects. After suicide rates caused by gambling habits spiked, the government enacted tighter laws. 

The law of Games of Chance and Casinos 2005 regulates online gambling in Armenia. It also provides for gambling operators, licensing regulations and procedures, and the regulatory requirements for casino operators. Article 4 of the law provides that commercial organizations registered in Armenia can operate online gambling platforms. This refers to the licenses operators must get from the Ministry of Finance. 

The gambling license legalizes their services in the country. Apart from the legalization, there are also some limitations. An example is gambling on blood sports, which are banned and considered animal cruelty.

History of Gambling in Armenia

Gambling has deep roots in Armenia, with its presence going as far back as the 16th century. In the times before Soviet rule, gambling was an unregulated activity conducted in diverse locations such as taverns, coffeehouses, and even churches.

During the Soviet Era 
When Armenia was under Soviet rule, all forms of gambling were prohibited. This ban was due to a combination of factors, including the communist government's intent to regulate economic activity, as well as its opposition to gambling, which they viewed as a vice.

After the Fall of the Soviet Union 
With the Soviet Union's collapse in 1991, gambling was given the green light in Armenia. Viewing gambling as a potential revenue source and a means to lure tourists, the government legalized it. Just two years later, in 1993, the first Armenian casino opened its doors.

The Current Scene 
Today, gambling is fully legal in Armenia. There are two established casinos in the country, both located in the capital city, Yerevan. The Ministry of Finance oversees the regulation of gambling in the nation.

In recent times, some concerns have been raised regarding the potential negative impacts of gambling in Armenia, like addiction and financial difficulties. On the other side, some argue that gambling can serve as a source of entertainment and an income generator for the government.

The future course of gambling in Armenia is yet to be determined. The government might introduce further regulations or even impose a complete ban on gambling. However, as it stands now, gambling is legal in Armenia and is a favorite pastime for many locals.
