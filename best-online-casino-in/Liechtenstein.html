Regulatory Framework: Gambling Laws in Liechtenstein

The Gambling Act of 2010 (GSG) stands as Liechtenstein's primary gambling regulatory law. Initially, GSG mandated casino licenses through a concession system. A license approval system was established in 2016.

The Gambling Act stipulates that gambling profits contribute to various charitable initiatives.

Oversight and Future Regulation

The Office of Economics oversees gambling activities. This division of the Ministry of Infrastructure, Economy, and Sport is responsible for many facets of the casino industry. Among those are permits, slot machine providers, and regulatory compliance.

The Office established a separate gambling department in October 2019. This unit is to regulate existing and forthcoming casinos, ensuring adherence to regulations.

Are Online Casinos Legal in Liechtenstein?

Liechtenstein permits online gambling operators through its licensing system. The Gambling Act regulates online gaming concessions.

Initially, the GSG introduced a concession system for licensing. In April 2016, it underwent amendments, transitioning to a license approval system. This allowed for a broader scope in the gambling market. The legislation includes online gaming operators, expanding beyond traditional brick-and-mortar establishments.

Though Liechtenstein allows online gambling, the licensing system is presently inactive. On November 19, 2019, the government suspended processing online gaming concession applications. The suspension supposedly lasts until the end of 2023.

Meanwhile, players can access international online casinos. Most online casinos in Liechtenstein operate from the outside. Local access to online casinos follows EU guidelines. That means that safety protocols are in place.

In December 2023, the government extended the inactivity of its licensing system. Due to this extension, new online gambling licenses will not be issued. Rendering online gambling is effectively not available legally in the country.
