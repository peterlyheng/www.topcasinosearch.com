Is Gambling Legal in Nicaragua?

Gambling has been LEGAL in Nicaragua since 2001. Currently, the act of 2014 governs the industry under the Ministry of Finance.

Is Online Gambling Legal in Nicaragua?

Online gambling has been LEGAL in Nicaragua since 2011. Even the original 2001 law allowed gambling. But, the rise of illegal activities made the government launch the new legislation.

Who Can Accept Players from Nicaragua?

In Nicaragua, licensed domestic online casinos and licensed land-based casinos accept players. Many licensed offshore online casinos welcome Nicaraguan players, too.

Is It Legal to Play Offshore Gambling Platforms in Nicaragua?

Yes. Since the government lifted the ban, Nicaraguan players can access foreign sites.

Can Tourists Gamble Online from Nicaragua?

Yep. As any kind of visitor, you can gamble online. The gambling laws apply to citizens and permanent residents.

Gambling Regulation in Nicaragua

Regulation and licensing in Nicaragua is a complex enterprise with several bodies involved. The management is split between:

  1. Board of Casino and Gaming Venues
  2. Ministry of Finance and Public Credit

Within the ministry, there are two relevant units:

  • Financial Analysis Unit
  • Treasury

Here's how it works:

The Ministry of Finance is in charge.

Its Financial Analysis Unit manages the Casino Control Council.

Its Treasury handles licensing.

The Board of Casino and Gaming Venues inspects machines and ensures fairness of outcomes.

Who Can Get an Online Gambling License in Nicaragua?

Companies that have permits for land-based casinos may acquire online gambling licenses.

What is the Legal Gambling Age in Nicaragua?

Nicaraguan players must be 18 years old to gamble online or offline.

Is There a Gambling Tax in Nicaragua?

Is There a Gambling Tax in Nicaragua?

Yes, but somewhat different than elsewhere. In Nicaragua, casinos pay fixed taxes per machine and table. There's no lump sum or ratio-based taxation scheme.

Do I Have to Pay Taxes on Gambling Winnings in Nicaragua?

De jure - yes. De facto - no. 

Income tax applies for the winnings, but for smaller-scale prizes, no taxing happens. If you get lucky and score big, like 100K or more, do pay the taxes.

Does that mean you can't gamble at crypto casinos? Not at all. Many Bitcoin casinos and other cryptocurrency gambling platforms accept players from Nicaragua.
