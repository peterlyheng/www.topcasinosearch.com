What are the gambling laws in Cyprus?

2. Gambling is allowed only in certain areas of the country

Gambling laws in Cyprus are strict and only allow sports betting. Land-based casinos can operate and offer online services, but all other forms of online gambling are banned. Banks are not allowed to transfer funds to and from banned websites. Eight international companies have received remote betting licenses and operate sportsbooks. Players can gamble at unlicensed casinos.

3. Casinos must follow all directives and regulations from the Gambling Commission of Cyprus

The Gambling Commission of Cyprus has issued a number of directives and regulations that casinos must follow, including:

  3. Advertising Code sets out the conditions and criteria set by the Authority and the advertising and promotion of casino games.
  4. Advertising Directive sets out the guidelines issued by the Authority for the advertising and promotion of casino games.
  5. The Betting Law 106 (i) 2012 defines the basic framework of activity in the field of betting in the Republic of Cyprus.
  6. The Lottery Law 1983 dictates the creation and framework of the National Lottery.
  7. The 1983 Lottery Regulations, which provide further details and specific information on the regulation and operation of the National Lottery.

6. Gambling is regulated by the government

The government regulations for gambling in Cyprus are very strict and highly regulated. All forms of gambling are effectively banned, except for sports betting, which is the only type of gambling allowed according to the Betting Act of 2012. The National Gambling Board licenses land-based casinos and international companies may apply for remote betting licenses (Class B). Although the European Union has pressured the government to legalize and liberalize the gambling market, only sports betting is legal, and no independent operators have been approved to date. Gambling laws in Cyprus are strict, with only online sports betting legal. Sports betting must be done at licensed online sportsbooks with a Class B license from the NBA, and players must be 18+. 8. Players must be aware of the tax laws of Cyprus

The tax laws of Cyprus for players vary depending on the type of gambling they are engaging in. History of Cyprus Gambling Law

The history of gambling in Cyprus started with the establishment of the State Lottery in the 1960s. Gambling was illegal until 1973, when the national lottery became legal. Despite being in the EU, the influence of religion limited the gambling market. In 2012, the Betting Act banned all CYP casinos except for sports betting, causing controversy but boosting OPAP's revenue. The 2015 Gambling Act targeted offshore websites. Currently, only sports betting is legal and regulated by the Cyprus Gaming Commission.
