Online Gambling Laws and Regulations in Pakistan

This section examines the legal side of online gambling in Pakistan. We'll find out whether online casinos are allowed, the minimum age for gambling, and how gambling winnings are taxed.

Is online gambling Legal in Pakistan?

In 1956, Pakistan became an Islamic republic, with Islamic teachings influencing many laws. Unfortunately, this created a problem for gamblers since gambling is seen as a sin under Islamic law.

Even before modern Pakistan, the Public Gambling Act of 1867 generally banned gambling. Over time, various laws applied to different regions. However, in 1977, the Prevention of Gambling Act replaced all previous laws. This made all betting and wagering criminal offenses. In 1979, General Mohammed Zia-ul-Haq, the military ruler, legalized tote betting on horse racing. This move was believed to gain support from politicians who owned racehorses.

Regarding online casinos, their legality in Pakistan is uncertain. Pakistani laws do not explicitly address online gambling, creating a legal gray area. Although there are no specific rules permitting or prohibiting online casinos, many Pakistanis gamble online without facing legal consequences. Players should stay informed about the laws and choose reputable, internationally licensed online casinos for a secure gaming experience.

What is the Legal Online Gambling Age in Pakistan?

In Pakistan, gambling is typically against the law, and the legal age for online gambling isn't clear due to uncertain regulations.

Do Players pay Taxes on Online casino winnings in Pakistan?

Because online gambling isn't legal, Pakistan does not tax players on casino winnings.
