    What is a VPN?

    A VPN is a Virtual Private Network. It is a relatively new way to connect to the internet that adds privacy and security to your browsing activities. The original creation of a VPN was industrial. Companies wanted to hide the information they send and receive from being tracked or intercepted by anyone. Later, these companies began to recognize the potential of this type of service for the general public. They started offering this service to everyone. Today the service is widespread, and anyone who wants to surf the internet privately can use a VPN. A VPN adds privacy primarily by changing the IP Address. The IP Address is the identification number of your device on the web. This number is available to anyone. However, if you use a VPN, the IP Address displayed will be different. On most VPNs, you can even choose what IP Address you want. For example, if you live in the US and want a UK IP Address, you can select that in the software configuration.

    Why do people need a VPN?

    People use a VPN for a tremendous amount of services and reasons. First, a lot of content on the web is restricted and hidden from us, based on our location. For example, some casinos sites are banned in the US. However, when you use a VPN, you can access them. Without a VPN, your IP address is visible, meaning that any entity on the web, such as – websites, search engines, authorities, social networks, can all see what you are doing. With the IP Address, they can also get some of your real-life information, such as the physical address, full name, financial information, and a lot more sensitive information. Many people use a VPN for their regular daily surfing. And others use it specifically for activities they want to be private.

    Common Uses of a VPN:

    1.  Hide your IP address – A VPN will hide your IP Address from anyone on the web. You can do anything you want, and no one can track your activity.
    2. Encrypt data transfers – A VPN can encrypt your information using the web. Whether or not your specific VPN offers encryption is something you need to check, although it is considered pretty standard. Why is encryption necessary? Let's say you are surfing your mobile from an airport. Suppose you want to check your bank account information and enter a password or other sensitive information. In that case, hackers surfing on the same WiFi network could potentially intercept this information, and you don't want that. In case they do, and you use a VPN, they would intercept encrypted details they can't use in any way.
    3. You can choose a specific location - If you want your shown location to change while browsing for whatever reason. A VPN can do so. Anyone from all over the world could appear as being located in the UK, for example, using a VPN.
    4. Enter blocked websites - Some governments block specific websites. However, they can't prevent people from other countries from visiting said sites. That's where a VPN comes to play. Utilizing a VPN, you can enter any websites you want.
