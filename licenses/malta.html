    What Is the Malta Gaming Authority?

    The Malta Gaming Authority (MGA) is a regulatory body that oversees gambling in Malta, including online casinos. It was set up in 2001 and operates under the Malta Gaming Act.

    MGA's main goal is to ensure that gambling activities are conducted fairly and transparently. They aim to protect players from fraud and other illegal activities.

    What specific criteria does MGA use to evaluate the so-called MGA-certified online casinos

    The MGA uses specific criteria to certify that these online casinos meet high standards of quality and integrity. These include:

    • Licensing Requirements: All the MGA casinos must meet MGA's strict requirements. This includes showing that their games are fair and random. It also involves proving their financial stability and adhering to anti-money laundering regulations.
       
    • Player Protection: The MGA prioritizes a fair gaming environment. They ensure player safety by checking that casinos have the following measures:

      1. Age verification procedures
      2. Responsible gambling tools
      3. Mechanisms for handling complaints and disputes.
       
    • Security Measures: MGA casinos must use efficient measures to protect players' personal and financial information. This includes using encryption to protect player data and ensure secure payments.
       
    • Game Fairness: The MGA checks that casinos use certified random number generators and that the games undergo regular testing. This ensures fair play and that casino games are not rigged or manipulated.
       
    • Responsible Gaming Policies: Licensed casinos must promote responsible gambling practices. These aim to ensure gambling is used only for entertainment purposes. They include tools and resources to help players who may have gambling issues such as addiction. Responsible gaming practices include but are not limited to:

      1. Offering self-exclusion options
      2. Setting deposit limits
      3. Providing access to support services.
