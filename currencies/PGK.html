    Brief Overview Of The Papua New Guinean Kina (PGK)

    The Kina is the official currency of Papua New Guinea. It was introduced in 1975 after the country gained independence from Australia. While it is not internationally recognized like other major currencies, the Kina plays a central role in Papua New Guinea’s economy and tradition. 

    Some of the key features of the Papua New Guinean Kina include:

    • Symbol: K
    • Subdivision: The Kina is subdivided into 100 toea, making it easy for smaller transactions.
    • Banknotes: Common denominations include K2, K5, K10, K20, K50, and K100.
    • Coins: Available in 1, 2, 5, 10, 20, and 50 toea, along with a K1 coin.
