    Europe Gambling Licensing Entities

    Gambling licensing entities are government agencies that regulate gambling activities. There are different European gambling licensing entities, and they vary based on each country. Popular licensing entities in Europe include:

    The United Kingdom Gambling Commission (UKGC):

    The UKGC is the body responsible for regulating gambling activities in the United Kingdom. It was created by the Gambling Act of 2005 and started to fully function in 2007. It has the role of issuing licenses, monitoring gambling operators like casinos, and establishing regulations. You can visit our UKGC online casinos guide for more details on its roles.

    Malta Gaming Authority (MGA):

    The MGA was established by the Lotteries and Other Games Act, and it became fully functional in 2004. It is the body responsible for overseeing gambling activities like online gambling, lotteries, sports betting, etc., in Malta. The EEA (European Economic Area) is an agreement that extends the EU market to non-EU member states. It allows players from all EU countries to play in MGA-licensed casinos unless it is specified explicitly in the law that they can't. More detailed information can be found on our MGA casinos page.

    Gibraltar Regulatory Authority (GRA):

    The GRA monitors and regulates gambling and other related activities in Gibraltar. It was created under the Gibraltar Regulatory Authority Act of 2000. The GRA is responsible for licensing and regulating gambling operators in Gibraltar. It also prevents criminal and unhealthy gambling practices from both players and gambling operators.

    The GRA monitors and investigates any online casino suspected of criminal activities. An example of this is money laundering. Then, it takes enforcement measures where necessary.

    Alderney Gambling Control Commission (AGCC):

    Alderney is located in the Channel Islands. The AGCC is a regulatory body responsible for monitoring online gambling operations in Alderney. This body was created by the Alderney e-Gambling Ordinance and started to function fully in 2006. It regulates online casinos in Alderney and ensures they adhere strictly to its regulations.

    The AGCC also investigates when an online gambling operator is suspected of breaching a regulation. Finally, it collaborates with other agencies to ensure that licensed online casinos and the general public do not engage in unhealthy gambling practices.

    Gambling laws in Europe

    The gambling laws in Europe are vastly different, and each country has a different body that regulates gambling. For example, the UK has the UKGC, Germany has the State Gambling Authority, and Sweden has the Swedish Gambling Authority.

    The European Economic Area (EEA) lets players from all countries play in Malta Gaming Authority licensed casinos, except the law specifically provides otherwise. Despite the differences, the countries share some laws in common, they include:

    1. Most European countries require gambling operators to be licensed before they can start to function. This license must be obtained from a regulatory body, and the operators must comply with the body's requirements. These include popular requirements like fair promotions and player protection. 
    2. Across Europe, the advertisement of gambling is prohibited. This is to prevent operators from attracting more players who are vulnerable and are likely to develop unhealthy gambling habits. It is also to protect minors.
    3. Although tax rates differ in each country, casino operators are taxed across Europe. Revenues and profits are taxed based on the profitability of the business.
    4. European players are heavily protected by the laws. As such, the gambling laws in Europe provide to that effect. European online casinos are mandated to take measures to protect players. These include responsible gambling tools, provision for support for people with a gambling addiction, age verifications, etc.
    5. Many European countries have different regulations for online gambling operators. Some provide different licenses and place them under strict monitoring, while others outright ban them.
