How to Manage Your Bankroll When Playing High-Stakes Slots

Written by:
Dave Tamir
Updated:
November 28, 2024
Table of contents

    High-stake slots offer you the chance to make substantial wins. But like most things that give high rewards, they also come with higher risks. Without a proper strategy, it’s easy to burn through your funds quickly. 

    Learning how to manage your bankroll is, therefore, crucial. It helps you play responsibly and extend your gaming sessions without unnecessary financial stress.

    Whether you’re a new player or a pro looking for a refresher, we have put together seven solid strategies based on our experience. Our goal is for you to have fun while gambling online without depleting funds. So, welcome and come along with us!

    Why Should You Manage Your Bankroll?

    Making the most out of online gambling is not just about how much you have but how you use it. The perk of playing high-stakes slots can quickly become a disadvantage if you don’t have a clear plan. It involves wagering larger amounts per spin, which means your potential winnings and losses are huge. 

    By managing your bankroll, you can:

    • Avoid overspending by ensuring you don’t exceed the set limits for yourself. 
    • Extend your gameplay by spreading your bets strategically to enjoy longer gaming sessions.
    • Maximise wins with bonuses and by adjusting bet sizes to capitalise on opportunities for bigger payouts.
    • Stay in complete control of your sessions without making impulsive decisions that can lead to disastrous losses.

    A well-managed bankroll turns high-stakes slots into a calculated, exciting challenge rather than a risky gamble. The rest of this article will show you how to do it effectively.

    The first and most important tip is to ensure you gamble at the right casino. Choose casinos that are reputable, fair to players, and hold valid licenses. The good news is that we are meticulous about selecting only the very best online casinos for players. You can read through our thorough reviews and additional sources to be sure the casino meets your standards.

    That being said, here are seven vital tips and tricks that will help you become a pro at managing your bankroll:

    Set a Budget

    Before you start playing at all, set a fixed budget and ensure you stick to it. You can do this by putting aside a certain percentage from your paycheck that you’ll use for gambling each month. Ensure this budget leaves you with enough money to cover all your living expenses. In short, only gamble with what you can afford to lose.

    To avoid the temptation of dipping into your living budget, try having a separate bank account for your gambling money. Alternatively, you can load the money on a prepaid card and don’t use more than that.

    After setting your gambling budget, choose slots with maximum bets within it. Divide your budget into wagers you can afford per spin. Bet sizes should be 2-3% of your bankroll. For example, with $200, set a max wager of $4 for 50 spins, $2 for 100 spins, or $1 for 200 spins. Adjust based on whether you want to extend gameplay, save funds for future sessions, or aim for bigger wins.

    Choose the Right Games - High RTP Slots

    The Return-To-Player percentage shows how much a slot game gives back to players over time. It is commonly represented by a percentage of your total bets. For example, a slot with a 95% RTP theoretically returns $95 for every $100 wagered. Slot games with high RTPs provide a higher chance of winning over time. We advise picking the ones with an RTP of over 96%.

    Understand How The Game Works

    High-stakes slots often have higher volatility than regular slot games. While you can win massive amounts with them, losses can also be disastrous, depleting your bankroll.

    Taking the time to understand precisely how the game works would go a long way in helping you develop a strong strategy. Start by familiarising yourself with both the game rules and also any additional rules from the online casino. Also, read the bonus features, paylines, and paytables to ensure the games fit your budget.

    Take Breaks in Between Bets and Practice Self-Control

    It can be tempting to keep playing when you have been on a winning streak or to chase your losses. But exercising discipline will help ensure you don’t go over your bankroll.

    Set out how much time you want to spend gambling per day/week and stick to it. Try using alarms to tell you when to stop playing or have an accountability partner. Also, take breaks in between gambling sessions by stepping away from your screen to stretch or do something else. Remember that gambling was intended for fun, not as a source of livelihood. 

    Other helpful steps in exercising self-control include:

    • Limit Autoplay Usage: If possible, turn it off completely. The autoplay feature lets you spin automatically. This speeds up gameplay and increases your chances to win by wagering more frequently. However, it also depletes your bankroll faster. Using manual spins would help to stretch your gameplay and make you think twice before going an additional game round.  
    • Pace Yourself Between Each Spin: In addition to using manual spins, try pausing between each spin. This strategy will help preserve your bankroll and extend your game sessions.

    Always take Profits 

    Having a winning streak isn’t the time to keep gambling with everything. This could lead to losing all your wins, including your capital, and being back to zero point. It’s important to always set aside a portion of your wins. Even if you win 10 times your bankroll, still gamble within your initial budget or add a small percentage to this at most. This way, even if you lose that round, you’ll still have your wins.

    Set a Stop Loss Point 

    Apart from your gambling budget, also set the maximum amount you can lose per session. As soon as you lose this amount, stop gambling and take a break. This tactic would help you avoid chasing losses and losing the money you could have used for future sessions.

    An additional tip is to keep track of your wins and losses, as this would help you manage your bankroll better. Keep a record of how much you started betting with and what you end up with after every session. Did you win? And how much? The same goes for losses. Taking these notes would help you see the bigger picture of whether you’re actually making money or depleting your bankroll.

    Conclusion

    And there you have it! Seven tried and trusted strategies that have helped us make the most of our bankroll. We hope you find them helpful, and we wish you the best of luck in all your gaming sessions!

    FAQs

    How much of my bankroll should I bet on a single spin?

    An excellent rule of thumb is to bet no more than 2-3% of your total bankroll per spin. For example, if you have a $500 bankroll, your maximum bet per spin should be between $10 and $15.

    Are high-stakes slots riskier than regular slots?

    Yes, high-stakes slots involve larger wagers, which means potential wins and losses are higher. Proper bankroll management is essential to enjoy the thrill without burning through your money.

    What’s the best way to recover from a losing streak?

    The best strategy is to take a break. The easiest mistake is increasing your bets, hoping to recover your losses. However, this would only lead to more losses. Instead, take a break, reevaluate your budget, and return to play only if you’re comfortable with the remaining funds.

    Does bankroll management guarantee wins?

    No. The point of managing your bankroll is to help you play responsibly, extend your sessions, and enjoy the experience while minimising risks. High-stakes slots are unpredictable, and outcomes rely purely on chance.
